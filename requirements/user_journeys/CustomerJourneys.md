# Customer Journeys

## Overview

This document outlines the key customer journeys for the athletic performance footwear MVP. These journeys represent the primary paths customers will take when interacting with our ecommerce platform, from initial discovery through post-purchase engagement.

## Journey 1: First-Time Shoe Discovery

### Persona: Alex, 28-year-old runner looking for new training shoes

**Trigger:** Alex heard about our new performance brand from a running forum

**Steps:**

1. **Homepage Discovery** - Lands on homepage, views hero section with featured athletic shoes
2. **Exploration** - Browses product categories, focuses on "Running" section
3. **Discovery** - Uses filters to find shoes for training (not racing)
4. **Evaluation** - Clicks on 2-3 products to compare specifications
5. **Consideration** - Reads detailed specs, looks at cushioning and support features
6. **Decision** - Selects preferred model and size based on size guide
7. **Purchase** - Adds to cart, completes guest checkout
8. **Confirmation** - Receives order confirmation email

**Touchpoints:**

- Homepage hero section and featured products
- Homepage navigation
- Category filtering
- Product detail pages
- Size guide
- Shopping cart
- Checkout process
- Confirmation email

**Pain Points to Address:**

- Need clear performance specifications
- Size accuracy is critical for athletic shoes
- Trust in new brand requires detailed product info
- Checkout should be quick and frictionless

**Success Metrics:**

- Time from arrival to purchase
- Number of products viewed before decision
- Cart abandonment rate
- Conversion rate for first-time visitors

## Journey 2: Performance-Focused Purchase

### Persona: Jordan, 35-year-old cross-training athlete

**Trigger:** Jordan needs specific training shoes for gym workouts

**Steps:**

1. **Homepage Entry** - Lands on homepage, immediately uses search bar for "cross-training shoes"
2. **Refinement** - Filters by training category and price range
3. **Comparison** - Views multiple products comparing weight and support features
4. **Validation** - Reads technical specifications carefully
5. **Selection** - Chooses based on specific training needs
6. **Purchase** - Completes purchase with saved shipping info
7. **Follow-up** - Checks order status via confirmation email

**Touchpoints:**

- Homepage with immediate search access
- Search functionality
- Advanced filtering
- Product comparison
- Technical specifications
- Saved customer information
- Order status tracking

**Pain Points to Address:**

- Need for detailed technical specifications
- Performance-specific filtering options
- Clear differentiation between similar models
- Easy reordering process for future purchases

**Success Metrics:**

- Search conversion rate
- Filter usage effectiveness
- Technical spec engagement
- Return customer rate

## Journey 3: Research-Heavy Evaluation

### Persona: Taylor, 42-year-old basketball player

**Trigger:** Taylor needs new basketball shoes and is evaluating multiple brands

**Steps:**

1. **Homepage Research** - Lands on homepage, explores featured products and brand value proposition
2. **Deep Research** - Spends time reading all product specifications
3. **Multiple Visits** - Returns to site several times before deciding
4. **Size Verification** - Carefully consults size guide and measures
5. **Final Decision** - Makes purchase after thorough evaluation
6. **Post-Purchase** - Expects order updates and delivery tracking

**Touchpoints:**

- Homepage brand discovery and featured products
- Category navigation
- Detailed product information
- Size and fit guides
- Multiple sessions across devices
- Order tracking
- Delivery notifications

**Pain Points to Address:**

- Comprehensive product information availability
- Consistent experience across multiple sessions
- Accurate sizing information
- Clear communication about order status
- Post-purchase support

**Success Metrics:**

- Session duration
- Return visit rate
- Research-to-purchase time
- Customer satisfaction post-purchase
- Return/exchange rate

## Key Journey Insights

### Common Patterns Across All Journeys:

1. **Performance Focus** - All customers prioritize technical specifications over aesthetics
2. **Size Sensitivity** - Athletic shoe fit is critical, requiring detailed size guidance
3. **Research Intensity** - Customers spend significant time evaluating options
4. **Trust Building** - New brand requires transparent product information
5. **Mobile Usage** - Research happens on mobile, purchase may occur on desktop

### Critical Success Factors:

- **Product Information Quality** - Detailed, accurate specifications are non-negotiable
- **Size Accuracy** - Getting sizing right prevents returns and builds trust
- **Homepage Impact** - Strong first impression drives engagement and trust
- **Checkout Simplicity** - Complex checkout loses customers who have already invested time in research
- **Mobile Experience** - Research phase must work seamlessly on mobile devices
- **Brand Credibility** - Professional presentation builds trust for new market entrant

### MVP Prioritization:

1. **Essential:** Product catalog with detailed specs, basic checkout, order confirmation
2. **Important:** Size guides, search functionality, inventory accuracy
3. **Nice to Have:** Homepage hero section, advanced filtering, saved carts, order history

### Post-MVP Enhancement Opportunities:

- Customer reviews and ratings
- Product comparison tools
- Advanced recommendation engine
- Loyalty program integration
- Performance tracking integration
